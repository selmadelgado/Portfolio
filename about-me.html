About Me

Long before media queries, social media and AI-generated everything, I was introduced to my first Macintosh, discovered PageMaker and fell in love with design, typography and creating things that looked good.

I became the Editor-in-Chief of my high school newspaper, spending countless evenings in the newsroom designing layouts, pasting up pages by hand with X-Acto knives and rubber cement. That same summer, I worked the night shift at Kinko’s in their graphics department, creating business cards, brochures, flyers, logos and marketing materials for customers from every kind of industry imaginable. I learned about branding, layout, design and typography and saw firsthand how design could shape the way people experience information. Most importantly, I discovered that I genuinely loved creating things.

During that time, I was selected for a summer journalism program with the Houston Chronicle, where I had the opportunity to work alongside editorial staff and further develop my passion for design, storytelling and visual communication.

At Kinko’s, while the day and evening crews took the orders, the night crew stayed behind to create them. I loved it so much that I often volunteered to work the day shift just to continue designing and learning.

Without realizing it at the time, I was building the foundation for everything I do today.

I loved the creative process, but what stayed with me most was seeing people react to something I had designed. Hearing someone say “wow,” “that’s beautiful,” or “I love it” after handing them a finished piece felt more rewarding to me than the paycheck itself. Somewhere along the way, I realized that when you genuinely love what you do, it stops feeling like work. That feeling — creating something that truly connects with people — has stayed with me throughout my entire career.

That same passion followed me into software development. In 2001, I joined ConocoPhillips as a Business Analyst supporting the Global Security department. What started as maintaining a department website quickly turned into something much bigger. I taught myself HTML, CSS and ColdFusion, built an in-house enterprise application and became deeply fascinated by the intersection of technology, systems and user experience.

During my 13 years at ConocoPhillips, I had the opportunity to work with some of the most incredible individuals and contribute to projects supporting the Global Security group around the world. I also traveled to various ConocoPhillips locations to provide technical assistance, training and support.

In 2015, our department was featured in the company’s Spirit magazine and I was honored to receive two Spirit Awards for my contributions to the company. I loved my job and at that time in my life, it was the best job ever.

What I’ve come to realize over the years is that my career has never really followed a straight line. It has evolved alongside technology itself — from print design and newsroom layouts, to web development, enterprise systems, digital marketing and now AI-powered creative workflows.

I’ve always been drawn to the space where creativity and technology meet.

That curiosity eventually led me to continue exploring design and development independently, complete the Full Stack Web Development program through the University of Texas at Austin, and begin building websites, applications and creative tools that blend storytelling, functionality and emerging technology.

Today, through 6000pixels, I focus on creative technology, AI-powered design, development and digital experiences that feel both human and modern. I still love learning. I still love designing. I still love building. And in many ways, I feel like I’m just getting started.

I still get excited about collaborating with creative people, solving problems, building beautiful things and creating experiences that make people stop and say, “wow.” Say Hello at hello@6000pixels.com
